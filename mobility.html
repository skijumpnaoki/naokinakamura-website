The Pledge

What we drive, we don't hide.
We measure it — and return all of it.

Over a year the van covers roughly 40,000 km across nine European countries — the winter World Cup, plus the summer Grand Prix (France, Poland, Romania). That's about nine tonnes of CO₂. Naoki purchases and retires JCX carbon credits for the full measured amount, and sends you a full report showing exactly how much was offset.

This is an effort working toward carbon neutrality. We don't claim to have achieved it — we measure what we emit and offset all of it with credits, and prove that fact in numbers.

Vision

Starting with the van. In time, all of the travel.

The first step is the van (≈ 9 t). The legs to Sapporo and Lake Placid are by air, where the van can't go. As MoveLogger — which measures travel automatically — matures, we'll extend the same system to all of the travel, flights and trains included. This season's partners are founding members of "the journey to take Naoki Nakamura's travel to zero."
